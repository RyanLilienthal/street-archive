The Street Archive of Walter Israel

Walter Israel, born on November 9, 1925, in Elmshausen, Germany, came of age in this village with his parents, Theodor and Mina Israel. Walterh's Jewish ancestors lived in Elmshausen for generations. According to Elmshausen's archives, the family's neighbors stopped socializing with the Israels following the National Socialist (Nazi) government's 1935 Nuremberg Laws. After the family's arrest from their home in on March 18, 1942, they were held in a transit camp in Darmstadt, Germany and subsequently deported to Poland. The deportation train stopped in Lublin, Poland where Walter was separated from his parents for forced labor in the Madjanek concentration camp. The fate of those who continued on the train, like Walter's parents, included temporarily living in the Piaski ghetto and followed by an evacuation to the Sobibor concentration camp, where they were murdered on arrival. Walter is listed on the July 10, 1942, crematory register for Majdanek.
