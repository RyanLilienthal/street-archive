The Street Archive of Walter Israel

Walter Israel, born on November 9, 1925, lived with his parents, Theodor and Mina Israel, in Elmshausen, Germany, the family's home for generations. According to local archives, the family's neighbors stopped socializing with the Israels in 1935, the year of the Nuremberg Laws. After the family's arrest from their home on March 18, 1942, they were held in a transit camp in Darmstadt, Germany and subsequently deported to Poland. The deportation train stopped in Lublin where Walter was separated for labor in the Madjanek concentration camp. The fate of those who continued on the train, like Walter's parents, included temporarily living in the Piaski ghetto followed by an evacuation to the Sobibor concentration camp, where they were murdered on arrival. Walter is listed on the July 10, 1942, crematory register for Majdanek.
